## Scoreline Picks

### Iraq vs Norway: 0:2 — Confidence: High

Norway are the heaviest favorite of the day outside of Argentina. Opta Supercomputer gives Norway 75.9% in 25,000 simulations, Kalshi traders push that to 82%, and the market (FanDuel -475, bet365 -450) agrees. Norway won all 8 UEFA qualifiers at 4.6 goals per game — the best-ever average for any European nation in a single qualifying campaign. Erling Haaland scored 16 qualifying goals, twice any other European player; Alexander Sørloth adds a second genuine penalty-box threat. Iraq are back at a World Cup for the first time since 1986 and only reached the tournament as the 48th and final qualifier via playoff. Opta's expected goals model puts Norway at 2.8 xG and Iraq at 0.9 xG per game.

Basis: Opta 75.9% Norway win; xG model 2.8 vs 0.9; market -450 to -475 Norway; PredictZ correct-score lean 0–2; WinDrawWin: Norway win, BTTS, over 2.5 — but Iraq's attack is rated just 0.8 goals/game vs. Norway, pushing the cleaner 2–0 outcome. Taking the conservative 2–0 over BTTS given Norway's defensive discipline.

Risk: Iraq drew Spain 1–1 in a pre-tournament friendly on June 4, showing they can defend and nick a goal. Haaland is playing his first World Cup — debut-night rust is a real (if small) risk. A 2–1 Norway is the main miss scenario.

### Argentina vs Algeria: 2:0 — Confidence: Medium

Argentina are the reigning World Cup champions and enter in the form of their lives — seven consecutive wins, one goal conceded in the last seven friendlies. Opta puts Argentina at 67.8% in simulations; Kalshi traders and Polymarket both sit at 71%. Dimers' model puts the most probable correct score at Argentina 2–0 at 14.1% probability, with Squawka and OddsShark aligning on the same scoreline. Argentina rank 2nd by World Football Elo (2,113 pts); Algeria are FIFA #28 but ranked significantly lower on Elo. Algeria's recent pre-tournament wins (12–0 combined vs Bolivia, Netherlands, Guatemala) came largely against weaker or rotated opposition.

Basis: Opta 67.8%; Dimers most-likely score 2–0 at 14.1%; Kalshi 71%; Squawka, OddsShark, CBS: all 2–0 Argentina; Argentina xG attack 1.9/game, xG defense 0.2/game; Algeria's European-based stars (Mahrez, Amoura, Gouiri) give them a ceiling but Argentina's defensive block is elite.

Risk: Argentina have started slowly at each of the last two World Cups (lost to Saudi Arabia 2022, drew Iceland 2018). Messi's first match after returning from a hamstring issue adds a touch of rotation uncertainty. Algeria beat a full Netherlands side in their pre-tournament friendlies. A 1–0 Argentina grind is the softer miss; a draw is the tail risk.

### Austria vs Jordan: 2:0 — Confidence: Medium-High

The clearest mismatch of the day. Opta gives Austria 69.6%, Kalshi 73%, FanDuel 75%. Dimers and Football Whispers both independently call Austria 2–0. Austria are FIFA #25 / Elo #22 with a 1.6 goals/game attack and 1.0 goals conceded. Jordan are Elo #82, conceding 2.4 goals per game and making their World Cup debut. Jordan are winless in their last five matches, conceding 2+ in each, and striker Ibrahim Al-Naimat has been ruled out injured. Austria have David Alaba, Marcel Sabitzer, Konrad Laimer, and all-time leading scorer Marko Arnautovic available.

Basis: Opta 69.6% Austria; Dimers and Football Whispers both 2–0 Austria; Jordan losing key striker to injury; Jordan 2.4 xGA per game; Austria Elo #22 vs Jordan Elo #82; FanDuel -260 Austrian money line. Note: Austria have only 1 win in their last 9 all-time World Cup matches — but that history is against top-tier opposition, not debutants.

Risk: Jordan scored 32 goals in qualifying (highest tally ever for them in a single campaign) and were Asian Cup runners-up in 2023 — they are not a complete pushover. Ali Olwan scored 9 qualifying goals. A Jordan goal is plausible; Austria 2–1 is a reasonable variant.

## Best Scoreline Leans

**Top pick: Iraq vs Norway 0:2.** Highest model confidence (75.9% Opta), biggest xG gap (2.8 vs 0.9), market at -475, and the most predictable outcome of the slate. Norway have too much firepower across the pitch for this to go wrong.

**Second pick: Austria vs Jordan 2:0.** Jordan's injury problems, debut nerves, and poor recent form make Austria the safest multi-goal favorite. Dimers and Football Whispers both name this exact scoreline.

**Third pick: Argentina vs Algeria 2:0.** Strong consensus, but the early-tournament slow-start trap keeps confidence at Medium. Take the pick but size it down relative to Norway and Austria.

## Traps / Upsets To Avoid

**Do not back Iraq.** At +1100 to +1200, the implied probability (7–9%) overstates their chance. Their xG attack against Norway is 0.9/game and Norway's defense in qualifying was near impenetrable.

**Do not take a Jordan win.** At +700, Jordan might seem tempting, but losing Al-Naimat and being winless with 2+ conceded in each of the last five matches makes this a pool-killer. The "debut motivation" narrative is not worth the exposure.

**Argentina cautious on overs.** Algeria beat Netherlands in a friendly — Mahrez and Amoura can cause problems. If you're picking a score, 2–0 is cleaner than 3–0 or 3–1. Argentina's recent defensive discipline (1 goal in 7 games) pushes against a high-scoring affair.

## Wait For Lineups

**Argentina vs Algeria** — Confirm Messi's fitness and starting role. He returned from a hamstring issue and started both warm-up wins (Honduras 2–0, Iceland 3–0), but World Cup opener intensity is different. If he starts in a deeper role or is rested, Algeria's chances of holding a draw improve.

**Austria vs Jordan** — Verify Arnautovic fitness and starting position (he's been managing knee issues at club level in the past year). His replacement Gregoritsch is nearly identical in odds (+125 vs +120 to score) but changes the attacking shape slightly.
