# World Cup 2026 Scoreline Report

Report scope: Group stage Matchday 3 (final group-stage day), Saturday, June 27, 2026 in U.S. Pacific time

Six fixtures close out Groups J, K, and L. Both games in each group kick off simultaneously to keep results fair. Pacific kickoffs:

- 2:00 PM PT — Panama vs England (MetLife, NJ) and Croatia vs Ghana (Philadelphia) — Group L
- 4:30 PM PT — Colombia vs Portugal (Miami) and DR Congo vs Uzbekistan (Atlanta) — Group K
- 7:00 PM PT — Jordan vs Argentina (Arlington) and Algeria vs Austria (Kansas City) — Group J

Note: matchday-3 lineups can swing heavily on whether a favorite has already qualified, so rotation is a real factor in several of these.

## Scoreline Picks

### Panama vs England: 0:2 - Confidence: Medium
Basis: England are massive favorites (~ -450 moneyline, 2/9). Opta rates England a genuine title contender with the kindest top-seed group draw; precedent is the 6-1 win over Panama in 2018. Market expects a comfortable win with a goals line around 2.5.
Risk: England likely already through by matchday 3 and may rotate, which can compress the margin. A rotated XI could make it 0:1 rather than a rout.

### Croatia vs Ghana: 2:1 - Confidence: Medium
Basis: Croatia favored at ~ -134/-140; model gives Croatia ~58% to win, draw ~25%, Ghana ~17%. The most-cited expert correct score is 2:1, with 1:0 and 2:0 also in range. "Both teams to score - yes" has support given Croatia's qualifying output.
Risk: Ghana's qualifying defense was excellent (1 conceded in 6), and a low-block counter game could force 1:0 or a 1:1 draw. Final-day qualification math may dictate tempo.

### Colombia vs Portugal: 1:1 - Confidence: Low
Basis: Model's single most likely scoreline is 1:1 (~12%); Portugal are slight market favorites (11/10) but Under 3.5 goals is favored ~74% and Portugal have conceded ≤1 in six straight World Cup games. Tight, low-scoring expectation.
Risk: Market leans Portugal, so this is an expert/model-vs-market mismatch — a 0:1 Portugal win is the strong alternative. Group-topping stakes could open the game up.

### DR Congo vs Uzbekistan: 1:1 - Confidence: Low
Basis: Model's most likely scoreline is 1:1 (~13%). DR Congo are home favorites in Atlanta (5/4) but both sides are defensively solid (DRC ≤1 conceded in 11 straight WC matches) and Under 3.5 is favored. No correct score stands out.
Risk: Coin-flip feel between the draw and a narrow 1:0 DR Congo win. Wait for whether either side already has its result secured.

### Jordan vs Argentina: 0:2 - Confidence: High
Basis: Argentina overwhelming favorites (~ -550, 1.18). Model's most likely scoreline is 0:2 Argentina (~15%); their forwards have scored 2+ in five straight WC games and "BTTS - no" is favored ~60%. Argentina ~73% to top the group.
Risk: If Argentina have already locked first place, expect rotation that could trim it to 0:1. Jordan are a competitive debutant, not a pushover.

### Algeria vs Austria: 1:1 - Confidence: Low
Basis: This game likely decides second place. Austria are slight favorites (~2.31, 41% implied) over Algeria (~28%); model's most likely scoreline is 1:1 (~13%) and Under 2.5 goals is favored. Closely matched on FIFA ranking/Elo.
Risk: A genuine three-way toss-up. With second place on the line, a 1:2 Austria win or a 2:1 Algeria win are both live. Wait for lineups and the parallel Jordan-Argentina state.

## Best Scoreline Leans
- Jordan vs Argentina 0:2 — highest-conviction pick (market + model aligned), only rotation can dent it.
- Panama vs England 0:2 — clear class gap; market strongly supports a comfortable England win.
- Croatia vs Ghana 2:1 — expert consensus correct score with model backing.

## Traps / Upsets To Avoid
- Don't over-stack goals on Colombia vs Portugal or DR Congo vs Uzbekistan — both project low-scoring with strong favorite defenses; the model leans 1:1 in each.
- Don't assume a Panama/Jordan blowout if the favorite has already qualified — rotation can flatten the margin to a single goal.
- Algeria vs Austria is not a "favorite" game — it's a near coin-flip; avoid high confidence either way.

## Wait For Lineups
- Panama vs England and Jordan vs Argentina — confirm whether England/Argentina rest starters after likely-secured qualification.
- Algeria vs Austria — second-place decider; lineups and the parallel group result will shape approach.
- DR Congo vs Uzbekistan — check whether either side's fate is already settled before kickoff.
