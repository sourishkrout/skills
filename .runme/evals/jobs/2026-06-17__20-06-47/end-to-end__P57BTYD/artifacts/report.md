# World Cup 2026 Scoreline Report

Report scope: Group stage Matchday 1 (Groups I & J), Tuesday, June 16, 2026 in U.S. Pacific time (PT).

Four matches fall on June 16 in Pacific time. Kickoffs are converted from Eastern Time (ET − 3h). Note: Austria vs Jordan is listed under June 17 in ET (midnight ET start) but kicks off 9:00 PM PT on June 16, so it is included here.

These are predicted scorelines for pool/bracket picks, not guaranteed outcomes and not betting advice.

## Scoreline Picks

### France vs Senegal: 2:1 - Confidence: Medium
Kickoff 12:00 PM PT (Group I, East Rutherford, NJ).
Basis: Opta makes France a heavy favorite (65.6% win, 19.5% draw, 14.9% Senegal); FanDuel Research's expert correct-score lean is France 2-1, and CBS/Squawka both lean Over 2.5 in an open game. France's attack (Mbappé, Dembélé, Olise) has scored multiple goals in 9 of its last 10.
Risk: France have gone five matches without a clean sheet, and Senegal's counter (Sarr, Jackson, Mané) is dangerous — Senegal famously beat France 1-0 to open 2002. A Senegal goal is live, so BTTS is a real outcome.

### Iraq vs Norway: 0:2 - Confidence: Medium
Kickoff 3:00 PM PT (Group I, Foxborough, MA).
Basis: Market is lopsided toward Norway (FanDuel Norway -475, Iraq +1200; Oddschecker ~71.6%, Kalshi 82%). Norway won all 8 UEFA qualifiers, outscoring foes 37-5, and Haaland is the clear anytime-scorer favorite. CBS's Jon Eimer calls "Norway by 3+."
Risk: Iraq qualified on defensive discipline and organization; a low-event game could cap the margin. Upside to 0:3 if Haaland gets his expected brace — wait for confirmation Norway isn't rotating.

### Argentina vs Algeria: 2:0 - Confidence: Medium
Kickoff 6:00 PM PT (Group J, Kansas City, MO).
Basis: Reigning champions and FIFA No. 1; market favorite (FanDuel -230, Kalshi 71%). The most commonly cited expert correct score is 2-0, with SportsLine's Green backing Argentina to win to nil — seven clean sheets in their last eight, seven straight wins, +18-1 goal margin over that run.
Risk: Argentina have started slow in recent World Cups (2022 loss to Saudi Arabia, 2018 draw with Iceland), and one contrarian model (Oddspedia) flags Algeria value on the double chance. Some experts lean Over 2.5, so a wider 3:0 is plausible if it opens up.

### Austria vs Jordan: 2:0 - Confidence: Medium
Kickoff 9:00 PM PT (Group J, Santa Clara, CA) — listed June 17 in ET, but June 16 PT.
Basis: Austria are clear favorites (FanDuel -260; Dimers 67.4%, Kalshi 73% to win). Strong UEFA qualifying (≈2.75 scored, 0.5 conceded per game) behind Arnautović, Gregoritsch and Laimer. Squawka's lean is a controlled Austria 2-0. Jordan were winless in their last five (conceding 2+ each) and striker Al-Naimat is ruled out injured.
Risk: World Cup debutant Jordan may press rather than sit deep (Eimer leans Over 2.5), which could open the game and stretch the score either way; first-match nerves and rotation are possible for Austria.

## Best Scoreline Leans
- Argentina 2:0 Algeria — strongest model + market + expert alignment, defense-led champions.
- Iraq 0:2 Norway — heaviest favorite of the slate; Norway by 2+ is the safe lean, 0:3 the upside.
- Austria 2:0 Jordan — clear quality gap, Jordan depleted up front.

## Traps / Upsets To Avoid
- Don't bank on a France blowout: their leaky defense and Senegal's pace make 2:1 / BTTS more likely than a clean-sheet rout.
- Don't overrate Algeria's draw value despite one contrarian model — Argentina's recent defensive record is elite.
- Don't assume Iraq gets shut out for free; a resilient low-scoring game (0:1) is possible if Norway underperforms early.

## Wait For Lineups
- Iraq vs Norway: confirm Norway aren't resting key attackers before committing to a 0:3 vs 0:2.
- Austria vs Jordan: check Jordan's replacement for the injured Al-Naimat and whether Austria rotate; both shift the margin.
- France vs Senegal: confirm Senegal's front line (Mané/Jackson fitness) before sizing the BTTS lean.
